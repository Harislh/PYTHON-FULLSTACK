World Championship is a layered geometric display sans built for stadium-scale lettering.

Each letter sits on a strict geometric skeleton — perfect circles, straight stems, even sidebearings — and the family ships in nine companion styles that stack at exactly the same coordinates: a Regular fill, a Bold and a Thin weight, an Inline that opens a hairline through the body, a Shadow and a WithShadow for depth, and three Fade variants that band the letterform in offset color stripes. Layer them and you get the inset-and-shadow chrome of an arena scoreboard or the chromatic banding of a televised fixture title; use any one alone and you get a clean, official-looking display sans.

It is the kind of lettering you can picture lit across a press-box wall the night of a final, or stamped along the side of a coach pulling into a depot at four in the morning.

The font is built with advanced OpenType functionality and guaranteed top-notch quality, containing stylistic and contextual alternates, ligatures and more automatic and manual features; all to give you full control and customizability.
It has extensive lingual support, covering all Latin-based languages, from North Europa to South Africa, from America to South-East Asia. It contains all characters and symbols you'll ever need, including all punctuation and numbers.
